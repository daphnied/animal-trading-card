Koala

Koalas are not bears. They are not placental or 'eutherian' mammals, but MARSUPIALS, which means that their young are born immature and they develop further in the safety of a pouch. It’s incorrect to call them ‘Koala bears' - their correct name is simply 'Koalas'.

  • Scientific Name: Phascolarctos cinereus
  • Lifespan: 13 – 18 years (In the wild)
  • Mass: 8.8 – 33 lbs (Adult)
  • Gestation period: 30 – 36 days

The koala is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia
